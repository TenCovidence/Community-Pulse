Executive Summary

This report provides an overview of the Community Team’s support activity through GrooveHQ inbox from July 2024 to June 2025. It focuses on trends in ticket volume, user queries across review stages, regional distribution, and common support topics.

Key insights include:

  • ⏱
    Response time performance remained strong, with a consistently low median response time that meets our service expectations.
  • 🔍
    Top queried review stages were identified, along with the most frequent types of user issues within those stages.
  • 🌐
    Regional ticket distribution highlighted geographic patterns in support demand, including the most commonly used email domains per region.
  • 🤖
    Chatbot (Helply) engagement data offered additional perspective on recurring support needs and user behavior in self-service contexts.

The analysis in this report will inform targeted improvements to the inbox support process and guide resource development efforts aimed at reducing preventable queries and improving user experience.

3. Looking Ahead FY26

What can we do better next quarter?
Stakeholders: All

The insights from this report will guide our next steps in FY26. Specifically, we will:

  • 📊

    Combine findings from this report with other key feedback sources

    Including webinar Q&As, webinar surveys, user conversations during our face-to-face interactions, Sales team and Academic Success team input, and knowledge base traffic analysis to:

  • –

    Identify gaps in our self-service resourcesi

  • –

    Prioritise updates and expansion of our knowledge base and webinar content

  • –

    Share recurring user pain points and wishlist items with the P&E team

  • 📅

    Generate this report quarterly

    To monitor emerging trends and track our progress over time.

  • 🗂

    Feed insights back into our workflows

    To ensure we are continuously improving our support offerings and resources.

These efforts will support our ongoing priority of maintaining a low median response time by enabling users to find what they need more quickly and reducing the volume of repeat or preventable queries.

i - Self-service resources are content, or tools that allow users to find answers or solve problems on their own without needing to contact support.
